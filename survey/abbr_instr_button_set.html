There is a collection of 1000 investment portfolios. Each investment portfolio contains 100 companies, and each company is either good (profitable) or bad (unprofitable). Each portfolio contains good companies and bad companies.

A portfolio is better than another one if it has more good companies. The following figure, which is called a histogram, depicts the distribution of the number of good companies in a portfolio for this collection of 1000 investment portfolios. 


This figure can be read as follows. The horizontal axis shows each possible number of good companies, from 0 to 100. For each possible number, the height of the corresponding bar depicts the number of portfolios that have that number of good companies (as shown on the vertical axis).

For example, there are 13 portfolios with 45 good companies  in this collection, and 40 portfolios with 50 good companies.
The portfolios you see in this experiment come from this collection.

This experiment consists of $${e://Field/num_rounds} rounds. Your tasks in each round consists of 4 stages.


Stage 1: Preparation Stage


When a round begins, the interface will select 2 portfolios that are RANDOMLY DRAWN from the collection of 1000 portfolios. 
  • One of the portfolios is better than the other (has more good companies).
Your job in a round is to JUDGE the number of good companies for BOTH of the portfolios, and choose one portfolio to INVEST in based on your judgment. 

The interface will represent each portfolio as a square grid of 100 dots, where each dot is either black or red.
  • The number of good companies of a portfolio is equal to the number of red dots in the square grid.
  • The location of the red and black dots are RANDOMLY DETERMINED.

Stage 2

 
  1. In the second stage, you will be shown HALF of the portfolios (whether the top or bottom halves are shown is random).
    • They will be shown for ${e://Field/seconds_shown} seconds.
    • You will report how many good companies you think are in each of the two half portfolios.
    • The interface will automatically calculate the predicted number of good companies by multiplying your guess for the half portfolios by 2.

Stage 3 and Stage 4


  1. In some rounds, you will make your investment decision in stage 3, after seeing the half portfolios. In other rounds, you will make your investment decision in stage 4, after you see both the half portfolios and the complete portfolios. You won't know when you will make the investment decision until after stage 2.
  2. Below are examples of COMPLETE portfolios. 
    • Portfolio A and B are the same portfolios as above (portfolios do not change throughout a given round).
    • They will be shown for ${e://Field/seconds_shown} seconds.
    • You will then report how many good companies you think are in each of the two complete portfolios.
  3. How to make your investment decision:
    • You will be able to invest in one portfolio each round.
    • Investing in the portfolio with more good companies will give you a higher payoff. This is explained in detail next.
  • At the end of the experiment, one round will be randomly selected to calculate your bonus payment.
  • In this selected round, if you successfully chose to invest in the better portfolio (i.e., the portfolio with more good companies), you will earn a bonus of $${e://Field/choice_bonus}.
  • One of the two portfolios in that round is randomly selected, and one of your guesses on the number of good companies in that portfolio will be randomly chosen. If your guess is no further than 5 from the true number, you will receive a bonus of $${e://Field/belief_bonus}.
    • For example, if the true number of good companies in the portfolio is 63, then you will receive $${e://Field/belief_bonus} if you report any number between (and including) 58 and 68. 
    • However, if your guess is any other number, you will receive $0. 
  • This payment scheme means that you should treat each round as if it would be used for payment, and try your best to make your best judgments and best guesses.
  • You will not receive feedback between rounds, and will learn your bonus details at the end of the experiment.
