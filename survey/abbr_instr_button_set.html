In the survey, you will be playing ${e://Field/num_rounds} rounds of investment games.


In each round, you will have limited time to look at 2 investment portfolios. Each portfolio includes 100 companies, represented by a grid of 100 dots. Red dots represent good companies, and black dots represent bad companies. Your job as an investor is to judge the number of good companies in each portfolio, and make investment decisions.


You have 3 opportunities to earn bonus money:

  • Making good investments
  • Scoring in the top ${e://Field/num_winners} in the investment games
  • Making accurate guesses about the number of good companies in the portfolios
The better you are at investing in these portfolios, the more money you will earn. You will learn more details later.

An investment portfolio (or portfolio henceforth) contains stocks from 100 companies, and each company is either good (profitable) or bad (unprofitable). Portfolios with more good companies will earn you more money than portfolios with fewer good companies.


For this experiment, we have constructed a collection of 1000 such portfolios. For each number of good companies (X-Axis), the following figure (called a histogram) tells you how many of the portfolios (Y-Axis) have that number of good companies. The average number of good companies for portfolios in this collection is 50.


The portfolios you see in this experiment will come from this collection of portfolios.

The interface will represent each portfolio as a square grid of 100 dots, where each dot is either black or red.


  • The number of good companies of a portfolio is equal to the number of red dots in the square grid.
  • The number of bad companies is equal to the number of black dots.
  • The location of the red and black dots are RANDOMLY DETERMINED.

In each round, you will encounter 2 investment portfolios. Each portfolio contains 100 companies, represented by 100 dots. Good companies are red dots, and bad companies are black dots.


When a round begins, the interface will select 2 portfolios that are RANDOMLY DRAWN from the collection of 1000 portfolios. Each portfolio contains 100 companies. The pair of portfolios are re-selected every round.


In a given round you will complete 3 tasks:

  1. See half portfolios, and Guess the number of good companies in the half portfolios
  2. See complete portfolios, and Guess the number of good companies in the complete portfolios
  3. Invest in a portfolio

To maximize your earning you should INVEST in the portfolio that you JUDGE to have more good companies.

The order of tasks 2 and 3 might differ in different rounds:

  • In some rounds, you will encounter Half Portfolios --> Complete Portfolios --> Investment
  • In other rounds, you will encounter Half Portfolios --> Investment --> Complete Portfolios
Therefore, please pay attention to the instruction in each round.

Bonus for Investment in Chosen Round

  • At the end of the experiment, one round will be randomly selected to calculate your bonus payment.
  • In this selected round, if your investment was successful (i.e., you invested in the portfolio with more good companies), you will earn a bonus of $${e://Field/choice_bonus}. If your investment failed, you will earn $0 bonus.

Bonus for Guess in Chosen Round

  • One of your guesses in the selected round will be randomly chosen for payment.
  • If your guess is no further than ${e://Field/belief_error_bound} from the actual number, you will receive a bonus of $${e://Field/belief_bonus}.

Bonus for Game Winners

  • At the end of the experiment, you will receive an investment score that is equal to the total number of successful investments you made (a number between 0 and ${e://Field/num_rounds}).
  • Participants with the top ${e://Field/num_winners} investment scores will win the game, and receive a $${e://Field/win_bonus} bonus each.

The bonus you earn will be calculated after you complete the experiment, and will be paid to you within one week of your completion.
